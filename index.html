Growth of Interstate Highway System Brings Reduced Traffic Deaths and Growing Cities

Gabriel Hartman ghartman@ucsc.edu

Purpose

In 1956, President Dwight D. Eisenhower signed into law the Interstate Highway Act, fundind a 41,000-mile network of highways across the United States. This has been called one of the best investments a nation ever made. The amount of money spent on the interstate highway system was returned in safety, economic gains, and improved quality of life for Americans. I want to show that federally funded investments to public infrastructure such as the Interstate Highway System can be cost effective and yield useful returns for the nation.
